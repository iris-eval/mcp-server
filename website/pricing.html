Frequently asked questions

Is the open-source version really free forever?
Yes. The self-hosted version is MIT licensed with no usage limits, no account required, and no features gated behind a paid tier. You can run it in production indefinitely at no cost.
What's the difference between self-hosted and cloud?
Self-hosted runs on your machine with SQLite storage. You manage everything. The cloud tier runs on managed infrastructure with PostgreSQL, adds team features (shared dashboards, alerting, API key management), and handles ops so you don't have to.
Why usage-based instead of per-seat?
Observability is a team concern. Per-seat pricing penalizes collaboration — it makes teams hesitate to give access to the people who need visibility. Usage-based pricing means everyone on your team can see what agents are doing, and you only pay for the volume of traces you ingest.
When will the cloud tier be available?
We're building it now. Join the waitlist to get early access and founding-member pricing. Waitlist members also get a direct line to shape what we build.
Can I migrate from self-hosted to cloud later?
Yes. We're building a migration path to import your SQLite traces into the cloud tier's PostgreSQL storage. Your evaluation rules and dashboard configuration will transfer as well.
Do you support EU AI Act compliance?
Iris's trace logging and evaluation capabilities directly support the traceability requirements in the EU AI Act (Article 14, effective August 2026). The Enterprise tier will include audit logs and retention policies designed for regulatory compliance.
